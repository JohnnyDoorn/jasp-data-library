Bayesian Sampling Workflow

Procedure

The objective of this sampling procedure is to show with 95% confidence that the amount of misstatement in the population is lower than the performance materiality of 5%.



The quantity of interest is the misstatement θ in the population. Misstatement is defined as the difference between an item's booked (recorded) value and its audit (true) value. When testing the population misstatement against a given performance materiality, two statistical hypotheses about θ are formulated:



The (null) hypothesis of intolerable misstatement H₊: θ ≥ 5%,

The (alternative) hypothesis of tolerable misstatement H₋: θ < 5%.



The audit risk α is the risk of incorrectly rejecting the hypothesis of intolerable misstatement H₊: θ ≥ 5%. To reject this hypothesis on the basis of a sample, the information from the sample must be sufficient to reduce α to an appropriately low level (i.e., α < 5%).



In a Bayesian analysis, the parameter θ is first assigned a prior probability distribution that incorporates the available audit information about the misstatement in the population. A description and figure of the current prior distribution can be found under the Report section. You can incorporate existing information using the options under the Prior section.

Planning

The purpose of the planning stage is to find a minimum sample size so that, given a certain number of expected misstatements, the sample provides sufficient information to achieve the specified sampling objectives.



The most likely expected error in the sample is expected to be 1. The minimum sample size that is required for a performance materiality of 5%, assuming the sample contains 1 errors, is 92. This sample size is based on the beta distribution, the a priori assumption that every value of the misstatement is equally likely, and the given expected errors.



Consequently, if the intended sample is evaluated and the sum of (proportional) misstatements in the audited items is lower than (or equal to) 1, the sample provides sufficient information to conclude that the misstatement in the population is below the performance materiality.

Selection

The purpose of the selection stage is to statistically select a number of sampling units from the population. Sampling units can be individual items (rows) or individual monetary units. The sampling units are selected from the population according to the selection method. To learn more about the current selection method, look under the Method section.



From the population of 100 non-randomized items, 92 sampling units (items) are selected from the ID variable ( Order.number ) using the fixed interval record sampling method.

Evaluation

The purpose of the evaluation stage is to infer the misstatement θ in the population on the basis of a sample.



The population consisted of 100 items and 100 units. The sample consisted of 92 sampling units, of which a total of 5 were misstated. The information from this sample combined with the information in the prior distribution results in a most likely misstatement in the population of 0.054 and a 95% upper bound of 0.11.

Conclusion

The objective of this audit sampling procedure was to determine with 95% confidence whether the misstatement in the population is lower than the specified performance materiality, in this case 5%. For the current data, the 95% upper bound for the misstatement is above the performance materiality.



The conclusion on the basis of these results is that the misstatement in the population is higher than the performance materiality.
