Results

Description:

This data set, "Stroop", provides response times of 19 participants in a 3 (Congruent, Incongruent, Neutral) by 2 (Stroop, Break) within subject design.

Variables:

All columns contain response times (RTs) measured in miliseconds.

Congruent-Stroop - RTs in the Congruent-Stroop design cell.

Incongruent-Stroop - RTs in the Incongruent-Stroop design cell.

Neutral-Stroop - RTs in the Neutral-Stroop design cell.

Congruent-Break - RTs in the Congruent-Break design cell.

Incongruent-Break - RTs in the Inongruent-Break design cell.

Neutral-Break - RTs in the Neutral-Break design cell.

This example JASP file demonstrates a divergence between the Frequentist and Bayesian repeated measured ANOVA for JASP 0.16.3. Before JASP 0.16.3 the Bayesian repeated-measures ANOVA did not include random slopes for all but the highest order repeated-measures interaction, but the frequentist ANOVA does include these random slopes.

In the results below, the frequentist ANOVA results shows that the effect of Congruency is significant (p < 0.05) while the effect of Preceding trial and the interaction between Preceding trial and Congruency are not significant (p > 0.05). The Bayesian ANOVA under the header: "legacy results without random slopes" illustrates the behavior of JASP before 0.16.3. Surprisingly, the Analysis of Effects table shows evidence in favor of Preciding trial (BFincl = 11.863), evidence against the interaction effect (BFexcl = 1/0.306 = 3.268), and undecisive evidence for the effect of Congruency (BFincl = 0.744). The Bayesian ANOVA under the header: "with random slopes" illustrates the behavior of JASP from 0.16.3 onward. The models considered all include random slopes. Now the results are in agreement with the frequentist ANOVA. There is overwhelming evidence in favor of Congruency (BFincl = 5429) but undecisive evidence for the effect of Preceding trial and the interaction between Preceding trial and Congruency.
