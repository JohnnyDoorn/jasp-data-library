Results

This is an example of the Bain-One-Sample-T-Test with dependent variable "postnumb" (a child's number score after watching Sesame Street for a year). The hypotheses that are evaluated are:

H0: mean postnumb = 30

H1: mean postnumb > 30

H2: mean postnumb < 30

The number 30 was entered in the input box labeled "Test value"

Note. Â The null hypothesis H0 with test value 30 is tested against the other hypotheses. H1 states that the mean is bigger than 30 and H2 states that the mean is smaller than 30. The posterior probabilities are based on equal prior probabilities.

BF01 = 24.715, that is, there is 25 times more support in the data for H0 than for H1. BF02 = 8.205, that is, there is 8 times more support in the data for H0 than for H2.

The Posterior probability shows the support in the data for H0, H1, and H2. As can be seen, the probability that H0 is the best of these three hypotheses is .86. This implies that a preference for H0 comes with a Bayesian error probability of .035 + .105 = .140.
