Results

This is an example of Bain-linear regression with dependent variable "funumb" (a child's number score measured one year after watching Sesame Street for one year) and predictors "prenumb" and "postnumb" (number scores measured at before and directly after watching Sesame Street for a year).

Two hypotheses are being evaluated. H1 states that each standardized (because the Standardize input box has been ticked) regression coefficient is equal to zero. H2 states that each standardized regression coefficient is larger than zero and that the standardized coefficient of postnumb is larger than the standardized coefficient of prenumb.

Note. Â BF.u denotes the Bayes factor of the hypothesis at hand versus the unconstrained hypothesis Hu. BF.c denotes the Bayes factor of the hypothesis at hand versus its complement. PMPa contains the posterior model probabilities of the hypotheses specified. PMPb adds Hu, the unconstrained hypothesis. PMPc adds Hc, the complement of the union of the hypotheses specified. All PMPs are based on equal prior model probabilities.

BF1c = 0, that is, there is no support for H1 in the data. BF2c = 4.09, that is, the support in the data for H2 is 4.09 times larger than the support for its complement "not H2".

PMP c shows the support in the data for H1, H2, and Hc (the joint complement of H1 and H2, that is, "not H1 or H2"). As can be seen, the probability that H1 is the best of these three hypotheses is .814. This implies that a preference for H2 comes with a Bayesian error probability of .000 + .186 = .186.

For each standareized regression coefficient the estimate, standard error and credible interval are presented. As can be seen, the coefficient of prenumb is only slightly smaller than 0 (and the credible interval also covers values larger than 0), the coefficient of postnumb is larger than zero, and the coefficient of postnumb is larger than the coefficient of prenumb. This is the main explanation why the data prefer H2 oover H1 and Hu.

Posterior Probabilities

The first plot shows a visual representation of the size of the posterior probabilities both without (on the left) and with (on the right) the inclusion of Hc. It can immediately be seen that in both cases the data prefer H2, that is, H2 is a better hypothesis than H1, and H2 is a good hypothesis because it is more supported than Hc. In the middle plot Hc is replaced by Hu. This plot was the predecessor of the plot on the right and should no longer be used.
