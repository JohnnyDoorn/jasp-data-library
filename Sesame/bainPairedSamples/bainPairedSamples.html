Results

This is an example of the Bain-Paired-Samples-T-Test with variables "postnumb" and "prenumb" (a child's number scores after and before watching Sesame Street for a year). The hypotheses that are evaluated are:

H0: mean postnumb = mean prenumb

H1: mean postnumb > mean prenumb

H2: mean postnumb < mean prenumb

Bain Paired Samples T-Test

Bain Paired Samples T-Test
  Hypothesis BF Posterior probability
postnumb - prenumb H0: Equal 0.000
H1: Bigger 2.762×10-43 1.000
H2: Smaller 276.005 0.000
Note.  The null hypothesis H0 with equal means is tested against the other hypotheses. The alternative hypothesis H1 states that the mean of variable 1 is bigger than the mean of variable 2. The alternative hypothesis H2 states that the mean of variable 1 is smaller than the mean of variable 2. The posterior probabilities are based on equal prior probabilities.

BF01 = 0, that is, there is much much more support in the data for H1 than for H0. BF02 = 276.005, that is, there is 276 times more support in the data for H0 than for H2. 

The Posterior probability shows the support in the data for H0, H1, and H2. As can be seen, the probablity that H1 is the best of these three hypotheses is 1.0. This implies that a preference for H0 comes with a Bayesian error probability of 0.
