Type 1 Instrument Capability

Instrument capability evaluation

Basic information about the study

The purpose of the study is to evaluate the variability of the measuring instrument itself. The instrument standard deviation (sg) results from the instability of the mechanics, positioning, pick‑up system and electronics. The strategy for evaluating measurements is to measure the same item in the same position more than once, thus reducing the influence of the product. The instrument variation is compared with 20% of the process variation (i.e. 6 sprocess) or with 20% of the tolerance. If the measurement system is used for other purposes than process control, or the process variation (i.e. 6 sprocess) is unknown, or the process has a Pp less than 1, the total specified tolerance is used in place of 6 sprocess.

This instrument capability study[1] has been performed in Schweinfurt at TWS Q1 for the bore grinding process. The measuring equipment that measures the bearings is gauge S-5441-0 / 17969. The parameter under study is the inner ring diameter (dm). The tolerance for this parameter (dm) is ‑15/0 µm. The reference value of the master piece (reference or xmaster) is -4 µm. The number of repeated measurements (N) is 50 readings. The repeated sample should be a calibration standard, an appropriate standard or a part with the best available quality. After taking a measurement, the operator removes the part and places it back again before taking the next measurement. All measurement results are recorded in their measurement sequence. The part has consistent orientation throughout the study.

The minimum acceptable instrument capability criterion is Cg and Cgk = 1.33.

The process variation (i.e. 6 sprocess) had been derived during a process capability study for that specific product or process assuming the process is in statistical control. The instrument bias is the positive difference between the reference value (xmaster) and the average (𝒙̅ g).


Data verification

A quick glance at the run chart and histogram will normally reveal any anomalous data. If found, it may be necessary to reconsider the validity of the study. In other cases it will be part of the conclusion of the study. It is recommended to assess the stability of the measuring system in a separate study.

Note: Only in exceptional cases should the number of measurements be less than 50 but under no circumstances, less than 25.

[1]   Data is taken from the SKF Quality Techniques, “QT 5 – Measurement systems analysis” (PUB GQ/P9 11765 EN – March 2012), the analysis is performed by René Klerx

Run chart

  • Enter all data for the study, such as gauge name or number, location, date of study, reference value, tolerance, resolution and individual actual measurements.
  • Create a run chart of the individual measurements.
  • The value of the measurement standard xmaster = -4.0 µm is plotted as a green line on this chart. The boundaries of the reference intervals xmaster + 0.1·T and xmaster – 0.1·T are displayed as a red line. The blue dotted and dashed line indicate the average of the measurement series 𝒙̅ g and the boundaries of the measurement system’s spread 𝒙̅ g + 3sg and 𝒙̅ g – 3sg. Compare the reference interval with the spread of the measurement system, the ratio is equal to the instrument capability index Cg.

Bias and Instrument Capability Table

Basic statistics

Bias is the absolute difference between reference value (xmaster = -4.0 µm) and the average of all measurement (𝒙̅ g = -3.946 µm) is 0.054 µm.

%Bias is the bias (0.054 µm) compared to the tolerance (15 µm) × 100% = 0.36%.

Instrument capability index Cg and Cgk are compared to 20% of the tolerance.

Cg = 0.2 × Tolerance / 6 sg = 3/(6×0.420015) = 1.19.

Conclusion: The measuring instrument has a poor repeatability, and needs improvement.
