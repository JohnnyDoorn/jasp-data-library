Benford's Law

Procedure

Benford's law states that in many naturally occurring collections of numerical observations, the leading significant digit is likely to be small. The goal of this procedure is to determine to what extent the first leading digits in the data set follow Benford's law, and to test this relation with a type-I error of 5%. Data that are not distributed according to Benford's law might need further investigation.

Conclusion

The p value is 0.46821 >= α and the null hypothesis that the first digits in the data set are distributed according to Benford's law is not rejected. The Bayes factor indicates that the data are 6899678 times more likely to occur under the null hypothesis than under the alternative hypothesis.
