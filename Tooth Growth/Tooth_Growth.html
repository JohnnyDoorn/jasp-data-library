Tooth Growth: Factorial 2 x 2 ANOVA

Description:

This data set, "Tooth Growth", provides "the length of odontoblasts (sllec responsible for tooth growth) in 60 guinea pigs. Each animal received one of three dose levels of vitamin C (0.5, 1, and 2 mg/day) by one of two delivery methods, orange juice (OJ) or ascorbic acid (a from of vitamin C coded as VC)." Data set included in R. Variables:

 * len - The length of odontoblast cells. 

 * supp - Supplement type (OJ = orange juice, VC = ascorbic acid). 

 * dose - The dose of the vitamin C (500 = 0.5 mg/day, 1000 = 1 mg/day, 2000 = 2 mg/day).

This example JASP file demonstrates the use of 2 x 2 ANOVA. Specifically, we assess the adequacy of the null hypothesis that neither type of supplement nor the dose of the vitamin C influence the length of the odontoblast cells.

References:

Bliss, C. I. (1952). The Statistics of Bioassay. Academic Press.

Crampton, E. W. (1947). The growth of the odontoblasts of the incisor tooth as a criterion of the vitaminc C intake of the guinea pig. Journal of Nutrition, 33: 491-504.
