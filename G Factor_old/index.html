G Factor: Exploratory Factor Analysis

Description:

This historical data set, "G Factor", provides Charles Spearman's data of pupils' school grades and sensory discrimination tests scores.

Variables:

  • Years - Age of the pupil in years.
  • Months - Age of the pupil in additional months on top of Years (e.g., the first pupil is 10 years and 9 months old).
  • Age - Age of the pupil in the decimal system.
  • Pitch - Score in pitch discrimination test.
  • Light - Score in light discrimination test.
  • Weight - Score in weight discrimination test.
  • Classics - School grade for classic studies.
  • French - School grade for French.
  • English - School grade for English.
  • Mathematics - School grade for mathematics.
  • Residuals [variable] - residuals of a [variable] after regressing it on Age.

This example JASP file demonstrates the use of exploratory factor analysis. Specifically, we examine whether the covariance structure of residuals of the 7 variables (Pitch, Light, Weight, Classics, French, English, and Mathematics) after taking Age into account can be explained by one general factor.

Reference:

Spearman, C. (1904): "General intelligence" objectively determined and measured. The American Journal of Psychology, 15: 201-292.

Data manually extracted from Table A., Experimental Series III., p. 289.

Scree plot

The scree plot with the Factor Eigenvalues shows a break at the 2nd factor. The 95% quantile of simulated data shows a little higher Eigenvalue than the 2nd  factor. Only the one eigenvalue of the 1st factor exceeds the eigenvalue from the simulated data - that is why the parallel analysis retrieves only one factor. If we had used the Kaiser criterion (Eigenvalues > 1), we would have selected a two factor solution.
