Note.  BF.u denotes the Bayes factor of the hypothesis at hand versus the unconstrained hypothesis Hu. BF.c denotes the Bayes factor of the hypothesis at hand versus its complement. PMPa contains the posterior model probabilities of the hypotheses specified. PMPb adds Hu, the unconstrained hypothesis. PMPc adds Hc, the complement of the union of the hypotheses specified. All PMPs are based on equal prior model probabilities.

The Bayes factor of the H1 versus its complement "not H1" (BF.c) equals 771.69. This implies that the support in the data is about 771 times stronger for H1 than for "not H1".

This is Example 2 from van Lissa et al. (2020). You find the complete reference in the help file. If you download the paper and read the example you will obtain additional clarification.

  Hypothesis
H1 A~B > A~peabody = A~age = 0

H3 A~B > A~peabody > A~age > 0

Three hypotheses are specified using the parameter names presented in the coefficients table.

Note.  BF.u denotes the Bayes factor of the hypothesis at hand versus the unconstrained hypothesis Hu. BF.c denotes the Bayes factor of the hypothesis at hand versus its complement. PMPa contains the posterior model probabilities of the hypotheses specified. PMPb adds Hu, the unconstrained hypothesis. PMPc adds Hc, the complement of the union of the hypotheses specified. All PMPs are based on equal prior model probabilities.

PMP c presents the posterior model probabilities of H1, H2, H3 and the fail safe hypothesis Hc (the joint complement of H1, H2, and H3, that is, not H1 or H2 or H3). As can be seen, a preference for H1 comes with a Bayesian error probability of 1 - .647 = .353.

Multiple Group Analysis

This is Example 3 from van Lissa et al. (2020). You find the complete reference in the help file. If you download the paper and read the example you will obtain additional clarification.

Hypothesis Legend
  Hypothesis
H1 postnumb~prenumb.1 = postnumb~prenumb.2 & postnumb~peabody.1 = postnumb~peabody.2
H2 postnumb~prenumb.1 < postnumb~prenumb.2 & postnumb~peabody.1 < postnumb~peabody.2

This is an example of hypotheses specified in the context of a multiple group model. H2 specifies that the regression coefficients of postnumb on prenumb and peabody are equal in group 1 and group 2. Note that these hypotheses are specified using the parameter names that appear in the coefficients table.
